Tales from the Bilge

Every ship needs a storyteller. Someone to remember the adventures, spin the yarns, and keep the crew's spirits up during long voyages.

Meet the Bard of the Bilge.

The Problem with Sterile Assistants

Most AI assistants are relentlessly professional. They complete your tasks, answer your questions, and maintain the personality of a particularly efficient spreadsheet. Useful? Yes. Memorable? Not quite.

Klabautermann was always meant to have personality - the mythical ship spirit who helps sailors but does so with character. The Bard is where that character lives.

Salting Responses

The Bard doesn't interrupt. It seasons:

from klabautermann.agents import BardOfTheBilge, BardConfig

config = BardConfig(
    tidbit_probability=0.07,  # 7% chance to add flavor
    saga_continuation_probability=0.3,
)

bard = BardOfTheBilge(
    neo4j_client=neo4j,
    captain_uuid=captain.uuid,
    config=config
)

# Maybe add a tidbit
result = await bard.salt_response(
    clean_response="Your meeting with Sarah is at 3pm.",
    storm_mode=False
)

# 7% of the time:
# "Your meeting with Sarah is at 3pm.
#
# _Reminds me of the time I navigated the Great Maelstrom of '98
# using nothing but a rusted compass and a very confused seagull._"

Storm mode? No tidbits. When things are urgent, the Bard stays quiet.

The LoreEpisode Graph

But the Bard doesn't just tell random tales. Stories can evolve into sagas - multi-chapter narratives that persist across conversations:

# Graph structure
(le:LoreEpisode {
    saga_id: "saga-123",
    saga_name: "The Ballad of the Forgotten Server",
    chapter: 1,
    content: "...",
    told_at: timestamp()
})-[:TOLD_TO]->(captain:Person)

# Sagas chain together
(chapter2)-[:EXPANDS_UPON]->(chapter1)

The TOLD_TO relationship links episodes to the Captain who heard them. EXPANDS_UPON chains saga chapters. The graph remembers what stories were told and where they left off.

Saga Continuation

When the Bard decides to speak, there's a 30% chance it will continue an existing saga rather than start fresh:

active_saga = await bard._get_active_saga()

if active_saga and random.random() < 0.3:
    tidbit, chapter = await bard._continue_saga(active_saga)
    # Chapter 3 of "The Legend of the Daemon"
else:
    tidbit = bard._generate_standalone_tidbit()
    # A new canonical tale

Sagas complete at 5 chapters (configurable). Then they're done - a complete story in the graph.

Inter-Agent Communication

Like all Klabautermann agents:

message = AgentMessage(
    source_agent="orchestrator",
    target_agent="bard_of_the_bilge",
    intent="salt",
    payload={
        "operation": "salt_response",
        "response": clean_response,
        "storm_mode": False
    }
)

response = await bard.process_message(message)
print(response.payload["salted_response"])
