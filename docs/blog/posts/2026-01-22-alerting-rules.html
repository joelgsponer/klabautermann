When Things Break

Metrics tell you what happened. Alerts tell you when to care.

Klabautermann already exports metrics to Prometheus. Now it knows when to wake you up.

The Philosophy

Not every problem needs attention. A brief latency spike? Normal. A sustained pattern? Investigate. A critical failure? Act now.

Three severity levels:

  • Critical: Service degraded. Immediate action required.
  • Warning: Potential issue. Investigation needed.
  • Info: Informational. No action required.

The Coverage

22 alerts across 6 categories:

Agent Alerts

AgentCriticalErrorRate: >25% errors for 2m (critical)
AgentHighLatency: P95 >5s for 5m (warning)
AgentCriticalLatency: P95 >10s for 2m (critical)

APIHighLatency: P95 >2s for 5m (warning)

The Routing

Alertmanager handles the routing:

route:
  receiver: 'default-receiver'
  group_wait: 30s
  group_interval: 5m
  repeat_interval: 4h
  group_by: ['alertname', 'severity']

  routes:
    - match:
        severity: critical
      receiver: 'critical-receiver'
      group_wait: 10s
      repeat_interval: 1h

Critical alerts go out in 10 seconds. Warnings wait 30 seconds to group. Info alerts batch aggressively.

Inhibition

If critical is firing, suppress the warning:

inhibit_rules:
  - source_match:
      severity: 'critical'
    target_match:
      severity: 'warning'
    equal: ['alertname']

You don't need to know it's critical AND a warning. You need to know it's critical.

The Setup

One command:

docker-compose -f docker-compose.monitoring.yml up -d

Three endpoints:

  • Prometheus: http://localhost:9090
  • Grafana: http://localhost:3000
  • Alertmanager: http://localhost:9093

Customization

The default config logs alerts. For production, wire up your channels:

receivers:
  - name: 'critical-receiver'
    slack_configs:
      - api_url: 'https://hooks.slack.com/services/...'
        channel: '#alerts-critical'
        title: '{{ .CommonAnnotations.summary }}'
        text: '{{ .CommonAnnotations.description }}'

Slack, PagerDuty, email, webhooks. Whatever you need.
