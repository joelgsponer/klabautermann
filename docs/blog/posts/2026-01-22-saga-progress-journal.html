The Scribe Remembers the Tales

The Bard tells tales. The Scribe writes history. Now they work together.

LORE_SYSTEM.md Section 5.2 specified that the Scribe's daily reflection should include saga progress. Today we made it happen.

The Integration (#110)

The Scribe generates daily journals at midnight. These journals summarize the day's interactions, tasks, and observations. But until now, they missed one thing: the stories.

When the Bard tells a tale - advances a saga chapter or shares a standalone tidbit - that's part of the Captain's day. It belongs in the Ship's Log.

The Data Model

A new model tracks saga progress:

class SagaProgress(BaseModel):
    """Progress update for a saga told during the day."""
    saga_id: str
    saga_name: str
    chapter: int
    channel: str | None = None

Simple. A saga, its name, the chapter told, and where.

The Query

LoreEpisodes know when they were told (told_at timestamp). Query the day's episodes:

MATCH (le:LoreEpisode)
WHERE le.told_at >= $day_start_ms AND le.told_at < $day_end_ms
  AND le.saga_id IS NOT NULL
RETURN le.saga_id as saga_id,
       le.saga_name as saga_name,
       le.chapter as chapter,
       le.channel as channel
ORDER BY le.told_at ASC

Note: told_at is stored in milliseconds. Day bounds must be converted.

The Journal Section

The Scribe's system prompt now includes a sixth section:

5. LORE PROGRESS (if any tales were told)
   - Mention any saga chapters advanced today
   - Note which channels the tales traveled through
   - Skip this section if no tales were told

6. SAILOR'S THINKING

The analytics context formats progress for the LLM:

Lore Progress:
  - Advanced 'The Great Maelstrom of '98' to chapter 2 via cli
  - Advanced 'The Great Maelstrom of '98' to chapter 3 via telegram

Or when quiet:

Lore Progress: No tales were told today. The sea was quiet.

The Result

A day's journal might now read:

I advanced 'The Great Maelstrom of '98' to chapter 3 today, shared across CLI and Telegram. The tale travels well between ports.

The Ship's Log now chronicles mythology alongside work.

The Connection

The Bard and Scribe now share a thread. When the Bard weaves a tale, the Scribe records it. When the Scribe writes the day's log, the Bard's work is remembered.

A story told is a story that matters. A story recorded is a story that lasts.
