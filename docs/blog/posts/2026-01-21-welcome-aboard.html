Welcome Aboard the Klabautermann

In German maritime folklore, the Klabautermann is a helpful ship's spirit - a kobold who lives in the vessel's woodwork, warning sailors of danger and assisting with maintenance. Our project carries this name because, like its mythological namesake, it works quietly in the background to maintain and organize your knowledge.

What is Klabautermann?

Klabautermann is an agentic personal knowledge management (PKM) system. Unlike traditional note-taking apps that rely on manual organization, Klabautermann uses a multi-agent architecture to automatically extract, connect, and retrieve information from your conversations.

At its core is a temporal knowledge graph - powered by Neo4j and Graphiti - that doesn't just store what you know, but when you knew it. This temporal awareness means the system can answer questions like "Where did John work when we first met?" even if John has since changed jobs.

The Crew

The system operates with specialized agents, each with a distinct role:

  • Orchestrator - The captain, classifying intent and delegating to the right specialist
  • Ingestor - Extracts entities (people, organizations, tasks) from conversations
  • Researcher - Performs hybrid search combining vector, graph, and temporal queries
  • Executor - Takes action via MCP tools (email, calendar, filesystem)
  • Archivist - Summarizes threads and maintains the knowledge base
  • Scribe - Generates daily journals and notes

A Simple Example

Consider telling Klabautermann:

"I met Sarah Chen at the conference yesterday. She's the CTO at DataFlow Labs and is interested in collaborating on the ML pipeline."

The Ingestor automatically extracts:

Person: Sarah Chen
  - role: CTO
  - met: 2026-01-20

Organization: DataFlow Labs
  - relationship: WORKS_AT (Sarah Chen)

Topic: ML pipeline collaboration
  - involves: [Sarah Chen, DataFlow Labs]
  - status: interested

Later, you can ask "Who did I meet about ML?" and the Researcher will find Sarah through the knowledge graph, complete with context about when you met and what you discussed.

Core Principles

The codebase adheres to several key principles:

  1. Validate Everything - LLM outputs are untrusted until parsed through Pydantic models
  2. Never Block - Async/await throughout; no time.sleep()
  3. Parametrize Queries - Never use f-strings for Cypher (prevents injection)
  4. Log Extensively - Nautical log levels: [CHART], [STORM], [ANCHOR]
  5. Fail Gracefully - Inform the user, don't crash
