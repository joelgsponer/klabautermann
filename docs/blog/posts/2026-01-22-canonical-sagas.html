The Five Sagas

The Bard of the Bilge has always had stories. Today, it has canon.

LORE_SYSTEM.md Section 4.1 specified five complete sagas - multi-chapter tales that unfold across conversations. Until now, they lived only in the spec. Now they live in code.

The Implementation (#102-106)

Five issues. Five sagas. One dictionary:

CANONICAL_SAGAS: dict[str, dict[str, str | list[str]]] = {
    "great-maelstrom": {
        "name": "The Great Maelstrom of '98",
        "theme": "origin",
        "chapters": [...],
    },
    "kraken-scroll": {...},
    "sirens-inbox": {...},
    "ghost-ship": {...},
    "lighthouse-passwords": {...},
}

Each saga has a name, a theme, and exactly 5 chapters. The structure mirrors how sagas work in runtime - the Bard picks a saga, tells chapter 1, and continues across conversations until chapter 5 concludes the tale.

The Great Maelstrom of '98 (#102)

Theme: origin. A tale of the early internet.

"I remember the Great Fog of '98. The bandwidth was so thin you could barely fit a 'Hello' through the wire."

Chapter 2 carries a JPEG across the Atlantic. Chapter 3 reveals it was a cat picture. Chapter 5 delivers the wisdom: "Sometimes the slowest journeys carry the most precious cargo."

The Kraken of the Infinite Scroll (#103)

Theme: battle. Fighting social media notifications.

"Every time I cut off a 'Like,' two 'Retweets' grew in its place."

The beast attacks at 2 AM. Fire doesn't work. Logic doesn't work. Only a library card defeats it - the Kraken hadn't seen a book in decades.

The Sirens of the Inbox (#104)

Theme: warning. The lure of urgent emails.

"They sing a song of 'Urgent!' and 'Immediate Action Required!' But it's all a ruse to lead your ship onto the rocks of burnout."

Four thousand spam messages deleted in one night. The Sirens respond with increasingly desperate subject lines. A quiet morning is worth any Siren's wrath.

The Ghost Ship of Abandoned Projects (#105)

Theme: melancholy. The haunting of unfinished work.

"The crew of that ship are all the 'I'll get to it later' promises. They whisper at night."

A TODO list from 2019. A README that was never written. Every cabin full of potential, gathering dust. Complete your tasks - keep the ghost ship at bay.

The Lighthouse of Forgotten Passwords (#106)

Theme: humor. Password recovery adventures.

"Its keeper is an ancient navigator named 'P@ssw0rd123'."

The vault holds every credential lost to "I'll remember it later." A password from 2012? Stored in a sticky note, which was stored in another sticky note. Use a password manager - disappoint the Lighthouse keeper.

The Structure

Each saga follows a narrative arc:

  • Chapter 1: The hook - introduce the situation
  • Chapter 2: Rising action - the conflict develops
  • Chapter 3: Complication - things get interesting
  • Chapter 4: Climax or revelation
  • Chapter 5: Resolution and wisdom

The themes span the Bard's emotional range: origin stories, battles, warnings, melancholy, humor. A complete palette for digital sea tales.

The Result

The Bard now has 5 complete sagas with 25 chapters of content. When a saga starts, it knows exactly where the story goes. When it ends, the Captain has heard a complete tale.

"Now I use a password manager. The Lighthouse keeper was not pleased, but my sanity was worth his disappointment."
